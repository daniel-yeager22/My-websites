My Top 5 Movies/Series

Alice in Borderland

Alice in Borderland was such a gripping and intense series. The storyline had me hooked from the very first episode — it’s not just about survival games, but also about how people react when pushed to their limits. I liked how the characters were developed, especially Arisu. You can really feel his growth from someone lost in life to someone fighting not just to live, but to understand the meaning of life. The visuals, pacing, and suspense were top-notch too. It honestly made me think about human nature and how far people will go when rules no longer apply.

Queen of Tears

Queen of Tears left a deep impact on me. The storyline was beautifully written — full of emotion, heartbreak, and hope. What really stood out was how it portrayed love not just in the happy moments, but through pain, sacrifice, and forgiveness. The chemistry between the leads was so natural, and the way the plot unfolded kept me emotionally invested from beginning to end. It’s not just a romance drama — it’ s a reminder of how fragile and powerful love can be when tested by life.

Avengers Infinity War

Avengers: Infinity War was one of the most intense and unforgettable movies I’ve ever watched. Seeing all the Marvel heroes come together in one film was epic, but what really hit me was how high the stakes were. The action scenes were insane, but the emotional weight behind each character’s decision made it more than just a superhero movie. And that ending… it left me speechless. Thanos was such a powerful and complex villain. This movie truly changed the game for the MCU.

One Piece Live Action

The One Piece live-action really surprised me in the best way. As someone who knows how beloved the anime is, I was a bit nervous at first — but the adaptation was done with so much respect and heart. The cast truly brought the Straw Hat crew to life, especially Iñaki as Luffy. The visuals, the fight scenes, and even the emotional moments were well-executed. It captured the spirit of adventure and friendship that makes One Piece so special. I'm honestly excited to see more!

Mouse Korean Drama

Mouse was honestly one of the most mind-blowing K-dramas I’ve ever watched. The plot twists had me questioning everything in every episode. It wasn’t just a crime thriller—it really dove deep into human psychology, morality, and how much our choices define who we are. sLee Seung-gi’s performance was incredible, especially how his character shifted throughout the story. This drama kept me on edge the whole time, and even after it ended, I couldn’t stop thinking about it.
